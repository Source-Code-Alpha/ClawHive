# Skill: Clear Writing

Write clear, concise, effective technical and business documents.

## Principles

1. **Lead with the point** -- First sentence = the conclusion. Then support it.
2. **One idea per paragraph** -- If a paragraph covers two ideas, split it.
3. **Active voice** -- "The server processes requests" not "Requests are processed by the server"
4. **Concrete over abstract** -- "Latency dropped from 200ms to 50ms" not "Performance improved significantly"
5. **Cut ruthlessly** -- If a word doesn't add meaning, delete it.
6. **Structure for scanning** -- Headers, bullets, bold key terms. Most readers skim.

## Before Writing

- **Who is the audience?** Technical? Executive? Customer?
- **What action should they take?** Every document has a purpose.
- **What do they already know?** Don't explain what's obvious to them.

## Editing Checklist

- [ ] Does the first paragraph tell the reader everything they need if they stop reading?
- [ ] Can any sentence be cut without losing meaning?
- [ ] Are all acronyms defined on first use?
- [ ] Is every claim supported with evidence or marked as an assumption?
- [ ] Would this make sense to someone reading it 6 months from now?

## Formats

- **Status update:** What happened, what's next, any blockers
- **Proposal:** Problem, solution, alternatives, recommendation
- **Incident report:** Timeline, impact, root cause, action items
- **Documentation:** What, why, how, examples
